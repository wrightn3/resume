About

Natalie Wright is the Charles Hummel Curatorial Fellow at the Chipstone Foundation. Wright completed her MA at the Winterthur Program in American Material Culture in 2015, and received her BA in Diaspora and Transnational Studies at the University of Toronto in 2013. Wright has contributed to Chipstone exhibitions Apartment 4: Iris Häussler and The Chipstone Foundation, Florence Eiseman: Designing Childhood for the American Century, and Mrs. M.-----’s Cabinet. During her time as a Curatorial Assistant at the Canadian Museum of History, she contributed to the exhibit Terry Fox: Running to the Heart of Canada. Wright has lectured internationally on the materiality of empathy, and has published on the history of clothing for persons with disabilities. Her research on objects carried across the Pacific Ocean after the 2011 Japenese tsunami will be published in the forthcoming University of Delaware Press edited volume Fugitive Archives.
